Portfolio Performance

Sep 2, 2018 4 min read

Portfolio Performance (Year-to-date) - Blue Line against Benchmarks in other colours. Starting index of 1000 at start of year.

Investment framework

The objective of the portfolio is to achieve the following against the benchmarks,

  • Highest daily win rates in positive periods
  • Highest sharpe ratio
  • Highest sortino ratio
  • Lowest max drawdowns against benchmarks
  • Shortest recovery to previous watermark
  • Positive alpha against all benchmarks
  • Low beta against benchmarks (<= 0.5): Target to maintain beta at around 0.15-0.25 range. May employ shorting of overvalued counters to achieve targets. Seek to reduce by beta by 0.15 during crisis (space out over X days (e.g. 5 days) - depending on hmm. Increase hedge count by 1 if crisis state. Reduce hedge count by 1 if non crisis state.)

Goal of my portfolio is to go against conventional wisdom of higher risk, higher returns through a risk parity approach. It’s entirely possible to engineer higher returns with lower risk (lower standard deviation, downward deviation and drawdowns).

My methodology is based on Quant Finance, Finance, Statistics and Economics. And heavily influenced by Ray Dalio and David Swensen.

Note that I’m not aiming for the highest absolute returns but for the highest risk adjusted returns!

I’ll add a counter to my financial goals for the month if I manage to achieve all the abovementioned criteria for year-to-date.

My asset allocation is similar to what Ray Dalio recommended in Tony Robbins book for an all season portfolio with an international tilt. The key is to first establish a stable baseline rate (another topic of the day from the book Superforecasting) of returns through asset allocation that can survive and thrive in different economic environments.

Then I overlay it with alpha strategies that reflect my view of the world in intermediate to long term to augment my returns.

Some of my alpha strategies are based on the following algorithmic toolkits (hope to add more over time),

  • https://jironghuang.github.io/project/what-is-low-regression-approach/ : Regressions of log price with prediction intervals
  • https://jironghuang.github.io/project/watch_list/ : ETF watchlist to identify
  • https://jironghuang.github.io/project/investment_compass_guidance/ : % from 52 week high vs expected returns 1, 2, 3 years later
  • https://jironghuang.github.io/project/hidden_markov_model/ : Regime identification through Hidden Markov Models & Simple Moving Averages to reduce conditional beta on low growth - low inflation enviroments

Leveraged portion in the portfolio is diversified across countries with low financing rates in Interactive Brokers (JPY, CHF, SEK, EUR, GBP)

Note:

  • As it stands, 100% of my net worth excluding CPF is devoted to this strategy. It aligns with my core belief that any analysis should be accompanied with skin in the game. There’s no room for armchair economist talk that is so prevalent in this environment. How you structure your portfolio should reflect your view of the world.
  • You may check my CPFIS returns and strategy instead here (https://jironghuang.github.io/project/xirr_cpf/)

Policy asset allocation

My ideal portfolio policy asset allocation is the following,

  • Developed Market Bonds: 40%
  • Developing Market Bonds: 10% (5% China, 5% rest of developing)
  • Commodities + Global Inflation Bonds: 20% (10% Commodities, 10% Global Inflation Linked Bonds)
  • Developed (ex real estate): 14%
  • Developing: 14%
  • Global Reits: 2%
